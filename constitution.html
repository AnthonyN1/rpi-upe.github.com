Renssealaer UPE Constitution

Article I. Name and Objectives

  1. The name of this organization shall be the New York Eta Chapter of Upsilon Pi Epsilon, or the Chapter.

  2. The purpose of this organization shall be the promotion of high scholarship and original investigation in the field of Computer Science.

  3. The New York Eta Chapter of Upsilon Pi Epsilon shall be located at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute (rpi.edu), in Troy, NY 12180.

Article II. Government of the Chapter

  1. The Chapter shall be governed by the Executive Council, made up of the Officers, members of the Cabinet, and all faculty members of the chapter. No individual may hold two positions on the Executive Council.

    Section 1. Officers of the Chapter

    1. The 3 Officers of the Chapter shall be the the Upsilon (President), the Pi (Vice President), and the Epsilon (Secretary).

    Section 2. Cabinet members

    1. The 5 members of the Cabinet shall be the the Historian, the Coordinator, the Recruiter, the Treasurer, and the Press Secretary.

    Section 3. Meetings of the Executive Council

    1. The Council shall meet at the request of any member of the Council. All meetings of the Council shall be chaired by the member who called the meeting, or his designee.

    2. Each member of the Council, including Officers, members of the Cabinet, and faculty members, shall have one vote for any ballots taking during a Council meeting. Votes shall pass by simple majority.

    3. Quorum necessary to conduct Chapter business during a meeting shall be two thirds of the voting student members of that group.

Article III. Membership of the Chapter

  1. The Chapter Recruiter shall call for at least one general meeting of the Chapter per semester, to report on the state of the Chapter's recruiting efforts. If there are any candidate members at the time of this meeting, the meeting shall act as a Member Elections meeting, and the Membership Committee shall present all membership candidates to the Chapter. For each candidate, a representative of the Membership Committee shall distribute a single page summary of the candidate's application, and shall speak on the candidate's behalf. A simple majority vote shall elect a candidate to membership.

Article IV. Officers and Cabinet Members

  1. Officers

    1. Upsilon (President)

      Upsilon's primary responsibility is to ensure all UPE actions and activities accomplish UPE's mission and ensure UPE's future success. Upsilon must constantly assess the direction of UPE, determining what changes need to be made to keep UPE on track with its mission and support the long term success of UPE. Upsilon should have basic understanding of all UPE actions and activities and passion for UPE's mission.

    2. Pi (Vice President)

      Pi supervises UPE's current projects and events, such as programming projects and recruiting activities. Pi's responsibility is to ensure the highest possible average success rate of projects by initiation (or cutting) projects, closely corresponding with project groups, and effectively distributing resource.

    3. Epsilon (Secretary)

      Epsilon manages UPE's internal and external communication, as well as UPE's infrastructure. Epsilon must manage or supervise the management of items that enable consistency of UPE across semesters, including meeting minutes, membership records, documentation of protocols and projects, and financial resources. Epsilon also oversees the Press Secretary in managing UPE's public face, enabling communication with external entitites.

  2. Cabinet Members

    1. Historian

      The Historian is in charge of maintaining UPE's bi-laws and ensuring all UPE activities are in accordance with them. The Historian must also ensure all UPE activities are in accordance with the National UPE constitution, and the bi- laws do not conflict with the National UPE constitution. The Historian is supervised by Upsilon.

    2. Coordinator

      The Coordinator's responsibility is to ensure no conflicts of resources or event times occur between UPE's activities. The Coordinator must also ensure no confclits occur between UPE and external activities, such as RPI-sponsored events. The Coordinator is supervised by Pi.

    3. Recruiter

      The Recruiter is in charge of UPE's membership recruitment efforts. Responsibilities of the Recruiter include organizing recruitment activities, managing applications, and facilitating the application reviewing process. The Recruiter is supervised by Pi.

    4. Treasurer

      The Treasurer is the manager of UPE's financial resources. The Treasurer is responsible to maintain a positive balance in UPE's accounts and allocate funding to proects. The Treasurer also makes financial recommendations, including the establishment of the semesterly dues fee. The Treasurer is supervised by Epsilon.

    5. Press Secretary

      The Press Secretary manages UPE's public image through a variety of communication media including email, posters, websites, and assemblies. All public announcements for UPE's activities must be managed or overseen by the Press Secretary. The Press Secretary is supervised by Epsilon.

Article V. Operating Rules

  1. All meetings of the Chapter, including meetings of its various Committees and sub-committees, shall have formal minutes taken, which shall include meeting attendance. Complete meeting minutes shall be sent to the faculty advisor within 5 business days of the conclusion of the meeting.

  2. Each Committee shall maintain its own set of by-laws to formally record its adopted procedures. These by-laws will be set by the individual committees, and may be changed by them as deemed necessary. All by-laws must be kept current with the Chapter Historian.

  3. Quorum necessary to conduct Chapter business during a meeting shall be two thirds of the voting student members of that group.

  4. The Chapter shall maintain one bank account for general disbursement. The faculty advisor shall authorize all withdrawals from the account. The account shall be filled in lump sum at the beginning of each Department budget cycle. The Finance Committee shall maintain formal procedures for requesting a disbursement in its by-laws.

  5. All rules of the Chapter shall be subordinate to the rules of this Constitution.

  6. For proper consideration, any proposed amendment to this Constitution shall be presented to the Rules Committee for formal framing, then to the Executive Council for discussion and formal adoption. The Rules Committee may not knowingly impede the propogation of a proposed amendment to the Council. However, no time limits shall be established for the framing process. The only exception occurs when the Rules Committee determines that a proposed amendment would be entirely superfluous or otherwise unnecessary for maintenance of the Chapter.

  7. The Chapter Recruiter shall call for at least one general meeting of the Chapter per semester, to report on the state of the Chapter's recruiting efforts. If there are any candidate members at the time of this meeting, the meeting shall act as a Member Elections meeting, and the Membership Committee shall present all membership candidates to the Chapter. For each candidate, a representative of the Membership Committee shall distribute a single page summary of the candidate's application, and shall speak on the candidate's behalf. A simple majority vote shall elect a candidate to membership.

  8. The Chapter recognizes the National Association's minimum requirements for eligibility, and shall rely upon the Membership Committee's by-laws to list exact eligibility procedures.

  9. All active members of the Chapter shall be considered to be voting members for Chapter and Committee business. Any student member who is a member of at least one Committee, and has been present at two of the past four general meetings, shall be considered active. Officers and faculty members are always considered active.

  10. All forms of Chapter elections shall be won by simple majority of the voting membership of the Chapter. Officers shall serve a term of one year from the month of that position's last election. The Chapter President shall call for at least one general meeting of the Chapter per year, to report on the state of the Chapter's overall activities. This meeting should occur during the last month of the Upsilon (President)'s term, and Officer Elections should be conducted as part of the Chapter business. Nominations for general membership should be made through the Membership Committee, as outlined in their by-laws. Nominations for an Officer or Cabinet position may be made by any voting member of the Chapter, though the current Upsilon (President) must make and announce his nominations for all current Officer and Cabinet positions at the beginning of the Officer Election meeting.

  11. Methods and Rules regarding Appoint of the Chapter Advisor:
    1. Election of the Chapter Advisor
      1. At the conclusion of each academic year, the Chapter shall hold a vote to re-elect the Chapter Advisor (the "Advisor"). Voting proceeds as a simple majority vote and, if successful, the Advisor will preside over the chapter for the following academic year.
      2. Otherwise, nominations shall be held by Present, Voting Members (the "Members") to select Candidates for the position of Advisor. Debate shall be held where the Members identify positive and negative items of each Candidate.
      3. Following discussion, a formal vote shall be held to select an Advisor-elect by majority vote.
      4. The Upsilon must contact the Advisor-elect, explain to him or her the duties entailed as the Advisor, and ask the Advisor-elect if he or she accepts the nomination.
      5. Upon the acceptance of the position of Advisor, the Advisor shall hold the office starting immediately and through the end of the current academic year. If the election occurs in the Spring semester after officer elections, the Advisor holds the position for the following academic year.
    2. Failure of the Advisor to perform Duties related to Chapter Business
      1. In the event that the Advisor is deemed no longer able to execute his or her dutities to the Chapter due to personal reasons, the Upsilon must hold a meeting to elect a new Advisor at his or her discretion. The meeting shall proceed as outlined in Article 5, Section 11, Subsection 1, Paragraphs 2-5.
      2. In the event that Members feel the Advisor has breached or failed to perform his or her responsibilities to the Chapter, the Members must call a formal vote at a Chapter meeting, and upon a simple majority vote, election of a new Advisor shall proceed as outlined in Article 5, Section 11, Subsection 1, Paragraphs 2-5.
  12. General Meetings shall follow the following format and rules:

    The Rensselaer Chapter of UPE shall meet a minimum of twice per month, during the academic year, to discuss current and future events as well as other issues concerning the organization. Meetings can be cancelled under extraordinary circumtances by Upsilon. Epsilon will take formal minutes of the entire meeting. The General Meeting will be held at the request of Upsilon and will follow the structure outlined below.

    1. The General Meeting is called to order by Upsilon
    2. Each officer (Upsilon, Pi, and Epsilon) will give a status update involving issues concerning his/her position
    3. Each cabinet member (Historian, Coordinator, Recruiter, Treasurer, and Press Secretary) will give a status update involving issues concerning his/her position and responsibilities.
    4. Each current committee will give a status update. This update will be given by the designated committee chair. If the committee chair is also an officer or cabinet member, he/she will speak for each occasion separately, once to discuss issues concerning his/her elected position and again to provide a status for the committee.
    5. After the status updates by the Officers, Cabinet Members, and Committee Chairs, the meeting will be opened for general issues and discussion.
    6. Meeting is adjourned by Upsilon.

    If, for any reason, Upsilon is unable to attend the meeting, another officer or cabinet member will head the meeting following this chain of succession: Pi, Epsilon, Historian, Coordinator, Recruiter, Treasurer, and Press Secretary. If for any reason, all officers and cabinet members are unable to attend, the meeting will be rescheduled for a different date.
